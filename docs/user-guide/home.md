# Home

The Home page is your dashboard overview — everything you need to understand your financial health in one screen.

## The score gauge

A single number from 0-100 with a letter grade. This is the weighted composite of seven category scores:

| Category | Weight | What it measures |
|----------|-------:|------------------|
| Cashflow | 20% | Income vs expenses, savings rate |
| Emergency Fund | 15% | Liquid savings as months of expenses |
| Debt | 15% | Debt-to-income, high-interest exposure |
| Goals | 15% | Feasibility with current surplus |
| Investments | 15% | Pension adequacy, risk alignment |
| Insurance | 10% | Life/income protection coverage gaps |
| Retirement | 10% | Income replacement projection |

A score of 70+ (grade B or above) means you're in solid shape. Below 60 (D or F), something material needs fixing — check the priority actions.

See [Scores & Grades](scores.md) for the full methodology.

## Key metrics

- **Monthly surplus** — net income minus all committed spending (expenses + minimum debt payments). Positive surplus funds goals and investments. Negative surplus means you're drawing down savings.
- **Net worth** — total assets minus total debt. The single best long-run proxy for financial health.
- **Savings rate** — percentage of net income being saved (basic). An "effective" version includes pension contributions.

## Priority actions

A ranked list of up to five actions, ordered by impact. Each action tells you:

- **What** to do (e.g., "Switch credit card balance to 0% transfer")
- **Why** it matters (e.g., "Saves £1,200/yr in interest")
- **How** to execute (e.g., "Apply for Barclaycard Platinum — 21 months 0%")

If you only act on one recommendation per week, these are the right five to start with.

## Re-analyse button

In the header, the **Re-analyse** button re-runs the full pipeline on your current profile. Use it after editing the Settings page or pulling in fresh bank data.

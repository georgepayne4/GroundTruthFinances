# Debt

This page answers three questions: how bad is my debt, which should I pay off first, and how long until I'm free?

## Summary panel

- **Total balance** — every debt added together
- **Monthly minimum** — what you must pay to stay current
- **Debt-to-income ratio** — total debt divided by gross annual income
- **Weighted average rate** — overall cost of your debt, weighted by balance
- **Total interest if minimum only** — lifetime cost of paying only minimums

If weighted average rate is above 10%, debt is the highest-return "investment" you can make. Paying down 20% APR credit card debt beats any risk-adjusted market return.

## Per-debt table

Each debt shows:

- **Balance** — current outstanding
- **Rate** — annual interest rate
- **Minimum payment** — monthly required payment
- **Months to payoff** — at current minimum payment
- **Risk tier** — High (>15% APR), Medium (5-15%), Low (<5%)
- **Avalanche priority** — rank for highest-rate-first strategy
- **Snowball priority** — rank for smallest-balance-first strategy

## Payoff strategy

GroundTruth recommends either **avalanche** (highest interest rate first) or **snowball** (smallest balance first) depending on your profile. Avalanche is mathematically optimal. Snowball has better behavioural momentum for people who need early wins.

If you have surplus to deploy, the recommendation tells you which debt to target with overpayments.

## Student loan intelligence

UK student loans are unique:

- Plan 2 (undergraduate, post-2012) — 30-year write-off, repayment 9% above £27,295
- Plan 3 (postgraduate) — 30-year write-off, 6% above £21,000
- Plan 1 (pre-2012) — 25-year write-off, 9% above £24,990

GroundTruth looks at your income projection and balance and determines whether you'll realistically clear the debt before write-off. If projected payments total less than the balance, the recommendation is **don't overpay** — you're paying a graduate tax, and voluntary overpayments are money lost.

The per-loan detail tells you the write-off intelligence verdict and reasoning.

## Credit card utilisation

Cards above 30% utilisation hurt your credit score and often signal cashflow stress. The dashboard flags utilisation tiers:

- Below 10% — excellent, optimal for credit score
- 10-30% — healthy
- 30-50% — warning, may impact score
- Above 50% — red, likely impacting score and a sign of cashflow tightness
